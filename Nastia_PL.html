Kal lay on the grass. The magpie looked at him, fluttered up, sat down next to the feces, and approached. A black pearl gleamed in an oily, chocolate-shagreen heap. Magpie sat down: the feces looked at her with one eye. Opening her beak, she squinted, tilting her head, jumped, pecked out a pearl and, holding it in the tip of her beak, flew away.

Soaring over the garden, the magpie glided along the hill, fluttered the willow and, hurriedly flickering its black and white wings, flew along the shore of the lake.

A reflected world floated in the pearl: black sky, black clouds, black lake, black boats, black forest, black juniper, black shallows, black bridges, black willows, black hill, black church, black path, black meadow, black alley, black manor , black man and black woman opening a black window in a black dining room.

Having finished with the window sashes, Sablin and Sablina lifted and placed on the window sill a large lens in a copper frame. Sablin turned it, focused the sun's beam on a cylindrical device, its lenses sent eight thin beams to all eight marks. NOMO, LOMO, SOMO, MOMO, ROMO, HOMO, KOMO and ZOMO flashed like polished golden hats, eight scattered, iridescent streams of light floated from them, crossed over the dish with the gnawed skeleton of Nastya, and in a second her smiling young face appeared in the air of the dining room and shone over the bones.
